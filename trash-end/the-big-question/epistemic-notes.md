# Epistemic notes for The Big Question

**Status:** Editorial review guide
**Scope:** All versions, with special attention to v13, v15, and the v16/v17 comparison

This page does not decide the scientific questions. It records where a reader could mistake a philosophical bridge for established evidence.

## Claims requiring the most care

### Planarian memory and regeneration

**Evidence:** In the 2013 Shomrat and Levin experiment, previously familiarized planarians showed evidence of faster behavioral recovery in a savings paradigm after decapitation and head regeneration.

**Do not infer automatically:** that a complete memory remained intact, that the experiment used a maze, that the information was stored in bioelectric fields or RNA, that a stable human-like self survives brain destruction, or that consciousness exists outside brains.

The version history improves here: v3 foregrounds RNA, v4 introduces distributed bioelectric patterns, v15 narrows the conclusion, and v17 separates behavioral memory, morphogenetic patterning, and persistent RNA interference.

### Bioelectricity, active inference, and “mind everywhere”

**Evidence:** Bioelectric signaling participates in development and regeneration; active inference is a formal modeling framework used across scales.

**Interpretation:** Terms such as “memory,” “goal,” “intelligence,” and “mind” can be operational at cellular scales without implying human-like awareness.

**Open question:** how far those operational meanings can travel before they become metaphor.

### Information, life, and autopoiesis

Information is not automatically computation, life, agency, or consciousness. A system that charges itself is energetically autonomous; a system that repairs itself maintains components; a system that reproduces makes copies. Autopoiesis concerns the continuing production and maintenance of the organization and boundary that constitute the system.

The essay should not use one property as proof of the others.

### AI consciousness

**Evidence:** AI systems perform increasingly complex information processing; researchers study machine consciousness as a serious question.

**Not established:** that present AI is conscious, that behavioral indistinguishability proves experience, or that greater scale necessarily crosses a consciousness threshold.

The Westworld comparison is an **analogy** and a philosophical pressure test, not experimental evidence.

### Evolution as a ladder

The sequence `RNA → cells → brains → us → AI` is useful as a narrative map. It should not be presented as a scientifically guaranteed direction toward greater awareness. Evolution does not promise a finish line or a universal climb in complexity.

### Assembly Theory and Constructor Theory

Both are real research programs. Using them together is an editorial hypothesis. Neither, alone or combined, proves substrate-independent consciousness, post-biological continuation, or the full bridge proposed by the essay.

### Penrose CCC, Landauer, and recurrence

Conformal Cyclic Cosmology is debated. Landauer's principle concerns the physical cost of logically irreversible information erasure. v17 states that using a lower Landauer bound as a physical route to a conformal reset does not follow: CCC does not use Landauer to produce its crossover.

Poincaré recurrence applies under specific mathematical conditions. Applying it directly to the real universe is not automatic.

### Digital and biological twins

A scan or 3D reconstruction is not by itself a biological digital twin. A stronger twin normally requires persistent identity, dynamic updating from the real counterpart, and a model used to monitor, simulate, predict, or intervene. NIST describes digital twins as representations tied to states and transitions, with forecasting as a foundational capability.

### Computronium

Computronium is a limiting idea about organizing matter for computation. Present AI hardware and data centers may be discussed as proto-computronic infrastructure by analogy and direction. They are not literal computronium, and computation does not establish consciousness.

## Editorial rule

Use the [epistemic-status convention](../concepts/epistemic-status.md) at section level, in notes, or at the first point where a category changes. Do not interrupt every paragraph with labels. The reader should be guided, not buried in disclaimers.
