# From molecules to minds: a testable map of multiscale agency

**Status:** working draft, not ready to send

I previously sent you *The Big Question* as a broad speculative map. This note isolates one branch that needed a narrower question and a way to fail.

## The distinction

Chemical complexity, life, agency, intelligence and consciousness should not be collapsed into one continuum simply because they share matter and information.

Erythrulose, a four-carbon sugar, has now been detected in interstellar space. Ribose and glucose have also been identified in pristine samples from asteroid Bennu. This matters to the origin of life's chemistry. It does not show that molecules are alive or intelligent.

At the next scales, however, cells maintain states, tissues repair form, and organisms learn. The question is not whether everything thinks. It is which observable competencies justify a change in vocabulary.

## The proposed test

TAME gives me a more useful starting point than a binary search for mind. I want to apply the same five questions at each scale:

1. What state or outcome does the system maintain?
2. How can that state be perturbed?
3. Can the system reach it through an untrained alternative route?
4. Does a past event change its future response?
5. What result would count against describing the behavior as agency?

The levels would begin with chemical systems as a baseline, then move through cells, tissues, organisms and designed or hybrid systems. The purpose is not to place them on a ladder. It is to prevent a word such as "intelligence" from doing work that an experiment has not earned.

## Why include chemistry

Chemistry would be included as a control boundary, not as a claim of molecular cognition. If a reaction produces order but shows no retained target, error correction or alternative path after perturbation, the framework should say so clearly.

This boundary may help separate continuity of material from continuity of competence. It also gives the biological cases a harder comparison than a purely philosophical definition.

## What may be worth developing

The contribution I am trying to test is modest: one falsifier-oriented matrix across scales, with explicit points where the evidence no longer supports the same term. It may turn out to be a restatement of work already contained in TAME or basal-cognition research. That is itself a useful result.

My current questions are:

1. Is this cross-scale test compatible with TAME, or does it remove distinctions that TAME needs?
2. What is the minimum empirical result that justifies moving from homeostasis to basal cognition rather than using cognition as a metaphor?
3. Can pre-cellular chemistry serve as the negative control for this map, or are there chemical systems for which the agency question is already experimentally productive?

I am developing this as a focused biological companion to *The Big Question*, not as another claim that every pattern is a mind.
