## Animal beat synchronization

| Primary source | What it supports | What it does not establish |
|---|---|---|
| [Patel et al. (2009)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2009.03.038) | Experimental synchronization to a musical beat in a sulphur-crested cockatoo | A universal animal capacity or human-like musical experience |
| [Cook et al. (2013)](https://doi.org/10.1037/a0032345) | Motor entrainment to rhythmic auditory stimuli in a California sea lion | That all non-vocal-learning mammals spontaneously keep a beat |

<a id="editorial-boundary"></a>
## Editorial boundary

The shared object is **temporally structured physical interaction**. Its consequences differ by system:

- a human listener may experience groove and move with it;
- some animals can align movement or signals in time;
- a plant can alter a physiological response after a relevant vibration;
- cells can coordinate collective dynamics.

The bridge to Computronium is therefore a declared analogy: biological information processing depends on timing and physical coupling, while computation can represent timing without necessarily reproducing embodied response or experience.
