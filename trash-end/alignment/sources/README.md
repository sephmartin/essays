# Alignment sources

**Status:** initial source map; not yet a quantitative claim ledger

This file separates empirical evidence, expert beliefs, forecasts, and the author's philosophical argument. None of the sources below provides a single objective probability that alignment will succeed.

## Current risk synthesis

### International AI Safety Report 2026

- [Report](https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/publication/international-ai-safety-report-2026)
- **Use:** current synthesis of evidence on general-purpose AI capabilities, loss-of-control risk, safeguards, and remaining uncertainty.
- **Supports:** advanced systems do not currently possess all the capabilities required for loss of control; risk would depend on future capability, misalignment, deployment conditions, and failed safeguards; present risk-management techniques remain limited.
- **Does not support:** a precise probability of extinction, successful alignment, or beneficial post-human continuation.
- **Evidence type:** multi-author scientific assessment.

## Expert-belief evidence

### Grace et al., *Thousands of AI Authors on the Future of AI*

- [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.02843)
- **Sample:** 2,778 researchers who had published in top AI venues.
- **Use:** demonstrates the dispersion of informed beliefs about capability timelines and social outcomes.
- **Supports:** expert opinion is highly uncertain and contains substantial disagreement; a majority assigned more probability to good than bad outcomes, while many still assigned non-trivial probability to extremely bad outcomes.
- **Does not support:** treating the median response as an objective, calibrated probability or as a substitute for a causal model.
- **Evidence type:** survey of beliefs, not observed outcome frequency.

## Quantitative protocol

For every numerical claim, the future essay should record:

| Field | Required entry |
|---|---|
| Event | Operationally defined outcome |
| Horizon | Date or interval |
| Estimate | Point, range, or scenario |
| Evidence type | Observed frequency, model, forecast, market, or elicited belief |
| Population | Systems, forecasters, experts, or cases represented |
| Dependencies | Variables that make the estimate conditional |
| Update date | When the evidence was last checked |
| Failure modes | Selection bias, ambiguity, model error, incentives, or missing base rate |

Capability, deployment, technical alignment, governance, misuse, survival, and beneficial continuation should remain separate rows. They may be connected through an explicit causal model later, but must not be multiplied as if independent merely because each has been assigned a number.
