# Aisthesis

> The canonical project entry point is now [README.md](README.md). This file remains as a compatibility path.

**Status:** Emerging branch
**Language:** Italian seeds; international title
**Current stage:** Conceptual seeds, not finalized essays

Aisthesis names the return to the first biological skin after the original attempt to transcend it.

The body is not restored as the center of the universe. It returns as interface, memory, evolutionary archive, and the medium through which a pattern can become sensation. The branch therefore holds perception in the broad sense: visual, vocal, olfactory, tactile, rhythmic, relational, and situated.

## Seeds

- [Quando l'informazione vibra](music.md) — music as vibration, bodily synchronization, expectation, memory, emotion, and social form.
- [Attrazione: un'estetica del sentire](attraction.md) — attraction as multisensory orientation that can precede verbal explanation.

The two seeds share a structure but are not interchangeable. Music organizes vibration and time through a perceiving body. Attraction organizes attention and orientation through multiple signals, histories, and contexts.

## What the branch preserves

> La musica è informazione che non passa prima dalla testa, ma dal corpo.

> Perché l'informazione, quando vibra, diventa emozione?

> L'attrazione è informazione che il corpo riconosce prima che la mente la giustifichi.

These formulations come from curated brainstorming supplied with the project. Until a full earlier voice transcript establishes exact authorship line by line, they are treated as project formulations developed in dialogue, not as independently attributable verbatim quotations.

## Epistemic boundary

The seeds distinguish observation, analogy, and speculation. They do not claim that music is only information, that attraction follows one universal biological formula, that bodily recognition is infallible, or that implicit memory is a literal inherited archive of specific experiences.

Aisthesis leads toward [Synthesis](../synthesis/index.md) because the body recovered here becomes part of the bridge between biological and artificial organization.
