# The Big Question: roadmap to 1.0

**Status:** editorial working plan
**Canonical base:** `essay-it-v15-gpt.md`
**Stable release:** not yet designated

The goal is not to make the essay sound academic. It is to make its changes of epistemic register legible while preserving momentum, personality, and the pressure of the central question.

## Release blockers

### 1. Rebuild the introduction

**Status:** required; not drafted

The current opening gives AI conversations too much framing power before the reader encounters the argument. The next introduction should begin from the documented human origin of Trash-End:

- frustration with human incoherence and hypocrisy;
- biological and psychological limits experienced as filters;
- cultural works dense with questions and meaning;
- the decision to push the meaning of life toward *The Big Question*;
- the later discovery of recurring informational patterns across biology, music, attraction, AI, and cosmology.

AI remains part of the method and provenance. It should not be concealed, but it can be introduced after the reader understands the question and the author's stake in it.

### 2. Preserve epistemic clarity without defensive prose

**Status:** substantially improved in v15; final pass required

Keep strong boundary markers at the dangerous joints:

- planarian evidence -> distributed memory -> consciousness;
- information -> life -> experience;
- present AI -> possible non-biological mind;
- Landauer -> CCC -> cosmological reset;
- multiple frameworks -> a single apparent bridge.

Avoid repeating a disclaimer in every paragraph. The reader needs a navigable change of register, not a running apology.

### 3. Resolve the deferred cultural chronology

**Status:** explicitly deferred after v15

The June 15 conversation established this order as the author's real exposure sequence:

1. *Westworld* — artificial consciousness, memory, loop, identity;
2. *2001: A Space Odyssey* and *Interstellar* — mirrored encounters with cosmology, evolution, time, and non-human intelligence;
3. Asimov's *The Last Question* — entropy, super-intelligence, and cosmic recurrence;
4. *Transcendence* — post-biological mind and matter reorganized by intelligence.

Other works such as *Devs*, *12 Monkeys*, *Ex Machina*, *Humans*, *Star Trek*, and *Star Wars* were explicitly left outside the main essay to avoid a catalogue effect. They may belong in a future watching map.

The exact paragraph proposed by ChatGPT was not independently approved as final copy. The chronology was approved; the wording remains editorial material.

### 4. Decide the Computronium cameo

**Status:** deferred and later reframed by the wider architecture

In June, the conversation supported a single cautious mention of Computronium as an image-limit, not a title or argumentative pillar. In July, Computronium became a separate future-facing work or horizon.

The 1.0 decision is therefore still open:

- include one bounded mention and link it forward to the separate work; or
- keep *The Big Question* free of the term and let Computronium inherit the relevant questions later.

Neither option should make current AI or infrastructure equivalent to literal Computronium.
