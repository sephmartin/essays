# The Big Question

> The canonical project entry point is now [README.md](README.md). This file remains as a compatibility path.

**Status:** Versioned working essay
**Stable release:** Not yet designated
**Languages:** Italian and English

*The Big Question* begins with anthropocentrism and follows a chain through biological memory, distributed organization, AI, entropy, and the question of why anything exists at all.

The essay is not one file pretending to be timeless. Its argument changes visibly across versions. Early drafts often move too quickly from information to consciousness or from mathematical analogy to cosmological possibility. Later versions increasingly expose those joints instead of hiding them.

## What the version history reveals

The history is not cosmetic:

1. v3 begins with strong claims about RNA, memory, informational life, AI consciousness, and cosmic recurrence.
2. v4 shifts the memory discussion from RNA alone toward distributed bioelectric and morphogenetic organization.
3. v5-v8 restructure the voice and expand the anthropocentric critique.
4. v9 introduces references.
5. v10-v12 soften several universal claims and add source provenance.
6. v13 attempts to connect Assembly Theory, active inference, Constructor Theory, CCC, and Landauer, but overstates several bridges and contains clear language defects in Italian.
7. v15 does not merely polish v13. It makes the essay bite itself: claims are marked as documented, debated, analogical, speculative, or possibly pattern projection.

The archive therefore treats v15 as current but not stable. A stable release should follow a claim-by-claim source review and an English rewrite aligned with v15's epistemic discipline.

## Relationship to the wider architecture

- The desire to escape the first biological skin belongs to **Trash-End**.
- The later recovery of body and perception opens **Aisthesis**.
- The attempt to relate biological and artificial organization without collapsing them becomes **Synthesis**.
- The sequence `RNA → cells → brains → us → AI` and the reverse speculative movement toward information create the horizon later named **Computronium**.

These branches emerged from the essay. They do not retroactively turn every sentence in every version into a settled part of the architecture.
